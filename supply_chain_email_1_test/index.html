Supply Chains in the Time of COVID

PART 1: What is the Supply Chain ?

It comes as no surprise to most that the COVID-19 Pandemic has had a wide-reaching negative impact on the business world. From unpredictable fluctuations in consumer demand, to material shortages, to labor scarcity, virtually every industry has had to contend with the challenges the pandemic has presented. That being said, there are few industries more directly impacted by COVID-19 than the Global Supply Chain. In order to understand these impacts, however, it's important to first discuss exactly what a supply chain is and what it does.

So what is a Supply Chain ? Well, to put it plainly, a supply chain is simply an interconnected network of individuals and companies working together to deliver a product to a consumer. These networks include producers; vendors; warehouses for storing goods; transportation companies to deliver goods; distribution centers; and the retailers and storefronts that stock these goods.

So, as the name implies, a Supply Chain is not any one company, business, or industry, it is an interconnected "chain" of processes and services all working in tandem to deliver a product or products to the consumer. Building on this analogy, if any single link in the chain experiences difficulties - or breaks entirely - the whole Supply Chain ecosystem will feel these effects. This is exactly what has happened to Global Supply Chains during the COVID-19 pandemic, and it's what will be discussed in greater detail in the following letter. Stay tuned !
